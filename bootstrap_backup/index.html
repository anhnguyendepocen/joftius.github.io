Joshua R. Loftus is a statistician who develops methodology for machine learning and data science pipelines with a focus on sources and types of error that were previously overlooked. This includes developing methods for inference after model selection that account for selection bias and analyzing the social fairness of machine learning algorithms from a causal perspective. His work has been published in the Annals of Statistics.

As a first generation college graduate, Loftus began his higher education in community college. He has participated in workshops at NYU on diversity and inclusion, and is happy to speak with, mentor, or help students from any background.
